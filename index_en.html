Vovinam Việt Võ Đạo is an martial art from Vietnam!

Vovinam (short for Võ Việt Nam; Vietnamese: Việt Võ Đạo, Martial Arts of Vietnam) is a Vietnamese martial art.
Vovinam is practiced with and without weapons. It is based on the principle of between hard and soft. It includes training of the body as well as the mind. It uses force and reaction of the opponent. Vovinam also includes hand, elbow, kicks, escape- and levering techniques. Both attack and defense techniques are trained, as well as forms, combat and traditional wrestling. The wide range of techniques include punching, kicking, forms, wrestling, sword, staff, axe, folding fan and others. (wiki)

The Vovinam Berlin Lichtenberg Club is a club inside the German Federation - DVVF

With great support from master Trần Đại Chiêu, Hồng Đai Đệ Nhị Cấp ( equivalent to 6 đẳng), vice president of the DVVF, and the generosity of the owner of the Driving School Hoan Kiem, Mr Cuong and Mrs Tho, and the support and donor from Mr. Hoang Vinh, the Vovinam Berlin Lichtenberg club was restarted.

Môn sinh Phạm Ngọc Hoàng - Hoàng Đai Vovinam (yellow belt)

Currently hoding a Yellow Belt (equivalent to other martial art as a Black Belt), he started learning Vovinam when he was still in Milan, Italy for studying at the end of 2010 with master Vittorio Cera (Hồng Đai Đệ Nhất Cấp, equivalent to 5 đẳng), technical director of the European Vovinam Federation.

In January 2016, he moved to Berlin with his wife to work in a startup in Berlin. During the training event of the European Vovinam Federation organized in Frankfurt in May 2016, he contacted master Trần Đại Chiêu, vice president of the German Vovinam Federation, and he helped setup the training of the martial art in Berlin Lichtenberg. The training has since taken place two times a week without interruption with a group of 5 - 7 practitioners.

From left to right: Phạm Ngọc Hoàng, together with master Trần Đại Chiêu, and Mon Sinh Vincent Heck, from SG Nied - Frankfurt club, in a trip to Geneve 01-03/07/2016 for the 4th European Vovinam Championship, co-organized by the European Vovinam Federation and Swiss Vovinam Federation
